# Why phone repair shops need a dedicated POS system

<time dateTime="2026-03-20" className="text-2xs font-medium text-gray-500">Mar 20, 2026</time>

If you run a phone repair shop, you have probably tried using a generic POS system at some point. Square, Shopify POS, Vend — they are all great for retail. But they were built to scan barcodes and sell products off a shelf.

Phone repair is different. Your workflow looks like this:

1. **Customer walks in** with a broken device
2. **You diagnose** the issue and give a quote
3. **You take in the device** and create a ticket
4. **Parts may need ordering** — the job is not done same-day
5. **You fix it**, then notify the customer
6. **Customer picks up** and pays

That is a service workflow, not a retail one. Generic POS systems have no concept of a repair ticket, device tracking, or job status. So you end up duct-taping things together — a POS for payments, a spreadsheet for tracking, maybe a paper ticket system.

## What a repair-specific POS gives you

A system built for repairs understands the full lifecycle:

- **Job intake** — capture device type, issue, customer details, and estimated cost in one flow
- **Ticket management** — see all active repairs at a glance, filter by status, search by customer
- **Status tracking** — move jobs through stages (diagnosed, waiting for parts, in progress, ready for pickup)
- **Customer notifications** — let customers know when their device is ready
- **Payment at pickup** — close the ticket and take payment in one step

## The cost of using the wrong tool

Every minute your staff spends switching between apps, retyping information, or hunting through spreadsheets is a minute they are not fixing phones or helping customers. In a high-volume shop doing 20+ repairs a day, that adds up fast.

The right POS system should feel invisible. It should make the next step obvious, not get in the way.
